# Selected Projects

## Rejection Simulator

A deliberately souless web toy that generates fake rejection emails.

I built it partly to understand how RimWorld assembles procedural descriptions, and partly as a questionable form of exposure therapy.

Learned: Procedural text generation, browser interfaces, and that jokes become less funny after testing them fifty times.

## Finding the Finger

A semantic-search experiment built around perennial philosophy.

I wanted to see whether similarities across religious and philosophical texts could be explored computationally rather than discussed only in vague abstractions.

Learned: Embeddings, data pipelines, web scraping, semantic search, and the difficulty of turning philosophy into a data model.

## Candidate Chess

A chess-training tool focused on identifying candidate moves.

I built it because I’m bad at middlegames and wanted something more targeted than solving random tactical puzzles.

Learned: Distributed processing, chess-engine analysis, queue systems, and how expensive it is to analyze a lot of positions.
